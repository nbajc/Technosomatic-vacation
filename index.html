Phase I
Venice Biennale

The 61st International Art Exhibition runs 9 May – 22 November 2026. Theme: In Minor Keys, curated by Koyo Kouoh's team after her passing — 110 artists, 100 national pavilions, 31 collateral events across the city. You have five days. This is how to use them.

★ Unmissable · Book in advance
Marina Abramović
Transforming Energy · Gallerie dell'Accademia · 6 May – 19 Oct 2026
First living woman artist in 250 years of the Accademia's history to be given a solo exhibition. Her performance practice — crystals, quartz beds, endurance works — set in direct dialogue with Titian, Tintoretto, Veronese. The centerpiece: her 1983 Pietà (with Ulay) placed face to face with Titian's last painting, the Pietà (1575–76). Abramović first visited Venice by train from Belgrade at age 14. She cried when she saw the city. She won the Golden Lion here in 1997. This is her full-circle moment at 80.
Gallerie dell'Accademia ↗
What to activate: The Transitory Objects — quartz beds, crystal-embedded structures — are interactive. You lie, sit, or stand on them to complete what Abramović calls an "energy transmission." This is the direct-body-contact thread running through her entire practice, from the early '70s performances to now.

Key works present: Imponderabilia (1977), Rhythm 0 (1974), Light/Dark (1977), Balkan Baroque (1997), new works created for Venice.

Practical: Timed entry required, book online. Weekends sell out. Allow 2–3 hours. The permanent collection galleries (Bellini, Carpaccio, Veronese) are worth an extra hour separately.

Where to stay · VRBO / Airbnb
Cannaregio is the call for this itinerary. Authentic residential sestiere, furthest from the tourist corridor. Canal-facing apartments on Fondamenta della Misericordia or Rio della Sensa. Walking distance to Palazzo Manfrin (Kapoor). 10-minute vaporetto to Arsenale and Giardini, 15 minutes to Dorsoduro. Search: "Venice Cannaregio canal view apartment" — look for piano nobile with exposed beams or terrace. Avoid anything on Lista di Spagna (tourist trap). Alternative: Castello near Via Garibaldi puts you closest to the Biennale venues on foot. Campo San Polo (San Polo sestiere) is more Rialto-central but requires more vaporetto time to reach the Biennale.

Phase II
Istria & Dalmatia

From Venice, drive east. The geography flows naturally: Treviso → Udine → cross the border into Istria → drive south through Croatia to Dalmatia and the Kornati archipelago. Ten days, four distinct worlds.

Day 6 · 30 min from Venice
Treviso
Medieval walled city with canals running through it. Tomaso da Modena frescoes at Santa Caterina. Prosecco country. Excellent aperitivo. One night before Udine.
Day 7 · 1 hr from Treviso
Udine
Friuli capital. Piazza della Libertà with Sansovino loggia and Venetian lion. Tiepolo frescoes at the Oratorio della Purità. Frico, cjarsons, Friulano wine. Gateway to Croatia.
Days 8–10 · 2.5 hrs from Udine · Istria
Rovinj
Terracotta old town on a peninsula — the most beautiful town on the Adriatic. St. Euphemia's church on the summit. Artist studios and small galleries in the cobbled streets. This is an artist's town. Day-trip to Pula (Roman Arena, Temple of Augustus, 40 min). Dinner at Konoba Veli Jože or Monte (Michelin starred).
Valalta Naturist · Nudist Beach ↗
Day trip from Rovinj · 40 min south
Pula
Roman arena — one of the six largest in the world, remarkably intact. Temple of Augustus in the forum. The Arch of the Sergii. Roman Istria at its most visceral. Come in the morning before the crowds and the heat.
Inland Istria · 50 min from Rovinj
Motovun
One of the great hilltop towns of Europe. Venetian walls crowning a forested ridge above the Mirna River valley — the view from the ramparts reaches the Adriatic on clear days. Medieval streets with almost no cars. The surrounding oak forests produce some of the world's finest white truffles; the Zigante truffle house in nearby Livade is worth the detour. The Motovun Film Festival happens in late July if your dates align. Drive it as a half-day from Rovinj or stay a night at the small hotel within the walls. Either way: go.

Day 11 · en route to Kornati
Šibenik
UNESCO Cathedral of St. James — 15th century, built without mortar from pre-cut stone. St. Nicholas Fortress at the river mouth. The base for your Kornati charter.
Day 13 · Kornati sailing
Telašćica
Dramatic cliff faces. The saltwater lake Mir — warm, still, turquoise, entirely enclosed. One of the most extraordinary swimming spots in the Adriatic. Anchor for the night, total silence.
Days 12–15
Kornati
Archipelago

149 islands · sailing · total solitude

The largest and densest archipelago in the Mediterranean. 149 islands, islets, and reefs — almost entirely uninhabited. Pale limestone cliffs rising from deep blue water. No noise. No road traffic. The occasional small tavern where fishermen grill fish caught that morning. George Bernard Shaw allegedly said God created the Kornati from tears, stars, and spirit on the last day of creation. He wasn't wrong. Charter from Šibenik or Zadar. Buy the National Park permit online in advance — significantly cheaper than from park rangers on the water.

Charter logisticsBook well in advance for summer. Bareboat if licensed (Croatian VHF cert). Skipper hire: €150–200/day — recommended if you want to focus on the islands, not navigation. Charter bases: Šibenik Marina or Biograd na Moru.
National Park permitRequired for the Kornati zone. ~€150–200 for 4 days if purchased online (kornati.hr) vs. double from park rangers on the water. Marinas: €80–150/night for a 40ft monohull.
When to goMay–September. June is ideal — maestral wind in the afternoons, calm mornings for swimming. July–August: busier, hotter, book everything earlier. The islands have almost no services — stock provisions in Šibenik before departure.
