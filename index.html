Phase I
Venice Biennale

The 61st International Art Exhibition runs 9 May – 22 November 2026. Theme: In Minor Keys, curated by Koyo Kouoh's team after her passing — 110 artists, 100 national pavilions, 31 collateral events across the city. You have five days. This is how to use them.

★ Unmissable · Book in advance
Marina Abramović
Transforming Energy · Gallerie dell'Accademia · 6 May – 19 Oct 2026
First living woman artist in 250 years of the Accademia's history to be given a solo exhibition. Her performance practice — crystals, quartz beds, endurance works — set in direct dialogue with Titian, Tintoretto, Veronese. The centerpiece: her 1983 Pietà (with Ulay) placed face to face with Titian's last painting, the Pietà (1575–76). Abramović first visited Venice by train from Belgrade at age 14. She cried when she saw the city. She won the Golden Lion here in 1997. This is her full-circle moment at 80.
Gallerie dell'Accademia ↗
What to activate: The Transitory Objects — quartz beds, crystal-embedded structures — are interactive. You lie, sit, or stand on them to complete what Abramović calls an "energy transmission." This is the direct-body-contact thread running through her entire practice, from the early '70s performances to now.

Key works present: Imponderabilia (1977), Rhythm 0 (1974), Light/Dark (1977), Balkan Baroque (1997), new works created for Venice.

Practical: Timed entry required, book online. Weekends sell out. Allow 2–3 hours. The permanent collection galleries (Bellini, Carpaccio, Veronese) are worth an extra hour separately.

Where to stay · VRBO / Airbnb
Cannaregio is the call for this itinerary. Authentic residential sestiere, furthest from the tourist corridor. Canal-facing apartments on Fondamenta della Misericordia or Rio della Sensa. Walking distance to Palazzo Manfrin (Kapoor). 10-minute vaporetto to Arsenale and Giardini, 15 minutes to Dorsoduro. Search: "Venice Cannaregio canal view apartment" — look for piano nobile with exposed beams or terrace. Avoid anything on Lista di Spagna (tourist trap). Alternative: Castello near Via Garibaldi puts you closest to the Biennale venues on foot. Campo San Polo (San Polo sestiere) is more Rialto-central but requires more vaporetto time to reach the Biennale.

Phase II
Istria & Dalmatia

From Venice, drive east. The geography flows naturally: Treviso → Udine → cross the border into Istria → drive south through Croatia to Dalmatia, the Kornati archipelago, and the island town of Trogir. Ten days, five distinct worlds.

Day 6 · 30 min from Venice
Treviso
Medieval walled city with canals running through it. Tomaso da Modena frescoes at Santa Caterina. Prosecco country. Excellent aperitivo. One night before Udine.

Day 7 · 1 hr from Treviso
Udine
Friuli capital. Piazza della Libertà with Sansovino loggia and Venetian lion. Tiepolo frescoes at the Oratorio della Purità. Frico, cjarsons, Friulano wine. Gateway to Croatia.

Days 8–10 · 2.5 hrs from Udine · Istria
Rovinj
Terracotta old town on a peninsula — the most beautiful town on the Adriatic. St. Euphemia's church on the summit. Artist studios and small galleries threading the cobbled streets. This is an artist's town: come for the light, the colour, the slow mornings. Kayak the harbour, take a batana boat at golden hour, dinner at Konoba Veli Jože or Monte (Michelin starred).

Golden hour
The Riva at Dusk
The waterfront at sunset, St. Euphemia's tower catching the last light. This is when Rovinj is at its most cinematic — an aperitivo on the rocks, the sky turning over the Adriatic.
Inland Istria · 50 min from Rovinj
Motovun
One of the great hilltop towns of Europe. Venetian walls crowning a forested ridge above the Mirna River valley — the view from the ramparts reaches the Adriatic on clear days. Medieval streets with almost no cars. The surrounding oak forests produce some of the world's finest white truffles; the Zigante truffle house in nearby Livade is worth the detour. The Motovun Film Festival happens in late July if your dates align. Drive it as a half-day from Rovinj or stay a night inside the walls — best at dawn, when the fog pools in the valley below and the town floats above it.

Days 12–13
Kornati
Archipelago

149 islands · 2 days sailing · total solitude

The largest and densest archipelago in the Mediterranean. 149 islands, islets, and reefs — almost entirely uninhabited. Pale limestone cliffs rising from deep blue water. No noise. No road traffic. The occasional small tavern where fishermen grill fish caught that morning. George Bernard Shaw allegedly said God created the Kornati from tears, stars, and spirit on the last day of creation. He wasn't wrong. Two days is enough for the essential loop — the core islands and Telašćica — before continuing south to Trogir. Charter from Šibenik. Buy the National Park permit online in advance.

Charter logisticsFor a 2-day sail, a skippered day-charter or short overnight charter from Šibenik works best. Skipper hire: €150–200/day — worth it to focus on the islands, not navigation. Bareboat possible if licensed (Croatian VHF cert).
National Park permitRequired for the Kornati zone. Buy online at kornati.hr in advance — significantly cheaper than from park rangers on the water. Priced per vessel length and per day.
When to goMay–September. June is ideal — maestral wind in the afternoons, calm mornings for swimming. The islands have almost no services — stock provisions in Šibenik before departure.
Days 14–15
Trogir

The journey ends on a tiny island town between the mainland and the island of Čiovo — a UNESCO World Heritage core of Venetian and Romanesque stone, walkable end to end in fifteen minutes. Trogir sits 30 minutes from Split airport: the natural place to close the trip before flying home.

⤢ Click to enlarge
Day 14 · the old town
Trogir Old Town
2,300 years of continuous habitation on one small island. The Cathedral of St. Lawrence with Radovan's Portal — the masterwork of 13th-century Croatian Romanesque sculpture. Kamerlengo fortress at the harbour. Marble streets, Venetian palazzi, evening passeggiata along the riva. Konoba dinners on hidden courtyards.
⤢ Click to enlarge
Day 15 · by boat from Trogir
Blue Lagoon & Blue Cave
The Blue Lagoon — shallow turquoise water between the Drvenik islands, glassy and luminous, perfect for swimming and snorkeling. Longer excursions from the Trogir–Split coast reach the famous Blue Cave at Biševo, where midday light floods the chamber and turns the water electric blue. A fitting final image: stone, sea, and impossible colour.
